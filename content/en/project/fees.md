## Do not collapse every flow into ‘commission’

A referral reward, delivery charge, payment-network cost, operator service fee, tax, and protocol contribution have different recipients and reasons. Combining them into a headline percentage hides who receives value and whether the charge is optional.

## Economic model

- Self-hosted software: independent operation does not require an optional managed-service subscription or a universal central-platform commission.
- Managed services: may charge for hosting, reliability, support, automation, or other named services; price and renewal terms must be shown before purchase.
- Payment and network costs: belong to the selected rail or provider and should be passed through or disclosed separately.
- Operator or seller charges: may be configured where lawful, but the responsible party and calculation basis must be visible.
- Referral or ecosystem rewards: must be funded, capped, attributable, and distinguishable from the seller's proceeds.
- Protocol/public-good funding: should be explicit, governed, and never implied by a draft percentage.

## Durable commitments

- Running Mobazha on infrastructure you control does not by itself create a fee owed to Mobazha for creating or completing an order.
- Open-source availability does not mean servers, networks, payment processors, plugins, taxes, support, or every optional service are free.
- Mobazha or another provider may charge for an identified optional service, but the provider, payer, value, price, renewal, and exit path must be visible before consent.
- A blockchain, payment-provider, plugin, tax, or exchange cost must not be described as a Mobazha platform fee unless Mobazha is the disclosed recipient.
- Self-hosted operation must not require enrollment in a Mobazha-operated managed transaction service.

## Required quote contract

- Name every charge and recipient.
- Show fixed and percentage components and the amount they apply to.
- State whether it is required, optional, refundable, or condition-dependent.
- Show the final total and settlement allocation before confirmation.
- Record the policy and quote version used by the order.

## Managed transactions, referrals, and agents

- A transaction-related service fee needs an identified service such as execution, automation, evidence handling, dispute operations, or a defined risk commitment.
- Referral or distribution compensation must be seller- or operator-funded, attributable to a settled transaction, capped, time-bounded, and reversible after refunds or fraud.
- An Agent or recommendation must disclose a paid relationship before influencing a paid decision.
- Multi-level recruitment payments, unlimited downstream percentages, and undisclosed paid recommendations are outside current Mobazha policy.

## Changing this boundary

Requiring a central Mobazha service or mandatory Mobazha fee for ordinary self-hosted orders requires a public RFC or ADR, migration and exit analysis, explicit client and API behavior, updated release scope, and applicable legal review. A provider may change an optional service price on its own pricing surface with an effective date, but that does not change this project-wide boundary.

> **Important:** Illustrative allocations in old discussions are not defaults. A percentage becomes policy only through public review, implementation, tests, disclosure, and release notes.
